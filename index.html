₹How pricing works — cost, margin, price

The single idea everything here is built on. Get this and the rate card reads itself.

We start from cost — what a resource actually costs us, fully loaded (salary plus overheads), expressed per month. We then choose a margin — the share of the client price we keep. The client price falls out of the two.

Price = Cost ÷ (1 − Margin)NOT Cost × (1 + Margin) — that under-prices every line

The distinction trips people up. A 60% margin does not mean "add 60% to cost." It means cost is only 40% of the price, so you divide by 0.40 — the price is 2.5× the cost. The higher the margin, the more aggressively price leverages off cost. Try it:

▤The margin ladder — why juniors carry more

Margin is not flat across levels. Junior roles carry a higher margin %, senior roles a lower one.

Two forces pull in opposite directions. Junior cost is low, so even a high margin keeps the absolute price reasonable and competitive — and junior supply is deep, so we can hold margin. Senior and specialist talent is scarce and expensive; we compress margin to stay winnable on price while the larger cost base still throws off healthy absolute rupees. So the ladder looks like this:

Niche +10% — Data Science, GenAI / Agentic, SAP S/4HANA, Workday HCM/Fin Mid +5% — Data Eng, DevOps, BAs, most functional consultants Standard — general engineering Entry −10% — annotation / data QC

Keep the premium honest. Niche skills carry a modest premium within reason — anchors stay L1–L3 $3,000–$6,000/mo, PM ~$8,000. A premium is not a licence to drift away from what the market actually pays.

⇄Two engagement models — don't conflate them

The L1/L2/L3 tier card and an hours-based INR/hr model answer different questions. Use the right one.

Tier model — L1 / L2 / L3

For pre-sale quoting, before CTCs are known.

  • Prices a resource per month off modelled cost and the margin ladder.
  • Lets you scope a pod and total a contract value before anyone is hired.
  • This is what the Rate Card and Quote Builder tabs run on.
Hours-based — INR / hour

For active engagements with known CTCs (e.g. the Cummins sprint model).

  • Bills actual hours against monthly sprint milestones.
  • Built from the real CTC of the person on the seat, not a model.
  • Cleaner for variable effort and FTE-conversion clauses.

◎Sourcing & triage — quality over years

Vendor fit and candidate integrity are where margin is protected or lost.

Match the vendor to the mandate. FTE mandates need FTE-capable agencies; contract-heavy vendors are weak fits for permanent roles. The Vendors tab tags every agency FTE / Contract / Mixed and Green / Amber / Pass so you don't send an FTE search to a temp shop.

Triage every profile before it reaches a client.

Green — submitAmber — clarify firstPass — do not send

Weight the quality of experience over raw years. Pressure-test AMS and agentic-AI claims — these are the ones most often inflated.

Integrity flags to surface proactively:

  • Resume inflation — scope or seniority that doesn't match the timeline.
  • Name / location / education mismatches across CV, profile and ID.
  • CV-factory patterns — near-identical bullet language across candidates from one vendor. Reject and give the vendor feedback.
◷
Submission rule. Anonymized profiles remove only the candidate's name — real organization and client names stay (Format B keeps the client name confidential, which is a separate thing).

⚖Government & sub-contracting — the hard disqualifiers

On government ERP pipeline work (e.g. as a Wipro sub-contractor), three filters knock bids out before capability is even read. Check them first.

  • 1
    Land-border country ownership (GFR Rule 144(xi)). Bidders with beneficial ownership from a land-bordering country need prior registration. Confirm the ownership chain is clean before investing in a response.
  • 2
    CERT-In empanelment / certification. Many bids require CERT-In empanelled security testing and ISO 27001. This is a near-term gap to close (VAPT vendor scoping in progress) and a recurring eligibility blocker.
  • 3
    Permanent payroll requirement. Some mandates require resources on permanent payroll, not contract — which disqualifies a staff-aug posture outright. Read the manpower clause early.
!
Know our lane. We're not positioned to lead national-level DPI architecture engagements, but we can own specific deliverables under a larger firm. Credible module claims: HRMS, Finance & Accounts, Procurement / Inventory.
